## Research Question

Can an evidence-grounded assistant reduce the time required to identify and communicate the primary cause of CTV campaign underdelivery without reducing operator trust or control?

## 30-Minute Interview

1. Tell me about the last campaign-delivery incident you investigated.
2. What first indicated that delivery was at risk?
3. Which systems, dashboards, or people did you need?
4. Where did you lose the most time?
5. What evidence did you need before changing a campaign setting?
6. Which actions require approval, and from whom?
7. What information can and cannot be shared with a client?
8. Review the dashboard and diagnose Campaign 1045.
9. Which result do you trust, doubt, or want to verify?
10. What would prevent you from using this during a live escalation?

## Usability Tasks

- Identify the highest-risk campaign without guidance.
- Diagnose Campaign 1045 and explain the leading cause.
- Find the evidence behind one root cause.
- Approve or reject a recommendation.
- Generate a client-safe explanation.
- Estimate whether the product creates enough value for a pilot.

## Measures

- Time to select the correct campaign.
- Time to first correct root cause.
- Root-cause agreement with expert label.
- Evidence opened or inspected.
- Confidence before and after using Signal.
- Recommendation approval errors.
- System Usability Scale score.

## Decision Rules

- Continue to pilot if four of five participants identify the correct root cause faster with Signal.
- Revise the evidence interface if fewer than four can explain why the top cause was selected.
- Do not automate campaign changes until approval comprehension is validated.
- Stop or narrow the concept if operators still need the same number of external systems to confirm a diagnosis.
